Spaghetti Recipe

Description

Spaghetti is a beloved Italian pasta dish featuring long, thin noodles tossed in a savory tomato-based sauce. The sauce is often made with ripe tomatoes, garlic, onions, and a blend of aromatic herbs like basil and oregano, simmered to bring out a rich and tangy flavor. Ground meat, such as beef or sausage, can be added for a heartier meal, or the sauce can be kept simple and vegetarian. The spaghetti noodles are cooked until tender, then mixed with the sauce, allowing the flavors to meld together. Topped with a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a drizzle of olive oil, spaghetti makes for a comforting, satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Onion
  • Tomato sauce
  • Black pepper
  • Garlic
  • Spices and seasonings
  • Noodles
  • Olive oil

Steps

  1. Cook the Spaghetti:Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.Add the spaghetti and cook according to package instructions (usually 8-10 minutes) until al dente.Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of pasta water, and set aside.
  2. Make the Sauce:In the same skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat.Add the chopped onion and cook until softened (about 3-4 minutes).Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1 minute until fragrant.Stir in the tomato sauce, crushed tomatoes, and tomato paste.Add oregano, basil, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using).If you cooked meat, add it back into the skillet now.
  3. Simmer the Sauce:Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. If the sauce becomes too thick, add a bit of the reserved pasta water to thin it out.
  4. Combine with Pasta:Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce and toss to coat the noodles thoroughly.If the sauce seems too thick, you can add more pasta water, a little at a time, until you reach your desired consistency.
  5. Serve:Plate the spaghetti and sauce, and garnish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves if desired.
